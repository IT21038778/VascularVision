Project Scope

Literature Survey

The VR Auscultation environment addresses critical issues in medical education by providing students with realistic heart sounds and accurate 3D models of the heart and body. This integration offers a seamless learning experience that bridges the gap between theory and practice, especially given the complexities of diagnosing cardiac conditions through auscultation. With limited clinical opportunities, students often lack exposure to a diverse range of cardiac issues, including rare ones, which hampers their ability to apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios. This study aims to connect theoretical understanding with practical implementation in cardiac auscultation, ultimately preparing healthcare professionals to diagnose cardiac conditions more accurately, leading to improved patient outcomes. In cardiology, coronary angiography is essential for detecting coronary artery disease (CAD) and other cardiovascular conditions. Traditional 2D imaging techniques can complicate interpretation due to their limited perspective. The AR Angiogram program utilizes advanced angiogram data to provide accurate recognition of the heart and blood vessels, creating a rich learning environment filled with tasks and challenges. By using an AR environment, the program helps students overcome the difficulties associated with interpreting angiograms and enhances their ability to apply classroom concepts to real-world scenarios. This innovative approach aims to refine medical students' understanding of cardiology, ultimately improving their diagnostic skills and clinical judgment.

Research Gap

Traditional methods of teaching cardiovascular conditions lack interactive, immersive experiences that simulate real-life diagnoses. Medical students often miss practical experience, which is crucial for learning how to recognize conditions like coronary artery disease and cardiac murmurs.

Several key research gaps have been identified in recent studies on ECG diagnostics and the application of AR and VR in medical education:

  • Real-Time ECG Analysis: Current solutions for ECG analysis often rely on post-processing techniques, failing to provide real-time feedback and instant diagnostic results. There is a need for applications that utilize machine learning and image processing algorithms to enable real-time ECG readings and diagnostics.
  • Augmented Reality Integration: While AR has been applied to map ECG lead placements on patients, its potential for interactive and real-time ECG data interpretation remains underexplored. Expanding AR applications to overlay diagnostic data on ECG reports could significantly enhance learning experiences for medical students.
  • Training in Complex Procedures: Existing educational tools for coronary angiography and cardiac auscultation lack comprehensive training components. There is a shortage of mobile-based AR solutions that focus on detailed training for these intricate procedures, which is essential for effective medical education.
  • Comprehensive VR Solutions: The integration of VR technology with coronary angiography outputs for educational purposes is limited. Existing research has addressed either coronary artery visualization software or VR environments for heart conditions but has not effectively combined these elements for enhanced learning.
  • Practical Application in Clinical Settings: The development of a mobile-based AR application for coronary angiography presents an opportunity to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application in clinical practices, thereby improving patient care and outcomes.

Research Problem & Solution

The Problem

The challenge we are addressing is the inadequacy of traditional medical education methods in providing realistic cardiovascular diagnostic training. Many medical students find it difficult to connect theoretical knowledge with practical skills due to limited clinical exposure. This often leads to a significant skills gap, especially in interpreting essential diagnostic tools like ECGs and angiograms, which are crucial for accurate diagnosis of complex heart conditions such as coronary artery disease, bradycardia, and tachycardia.

The Solution

Our proposed solution is VascularVision, a revolutionary mixed reality teaching and learning environment that combines Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) technologies. VascularVision aims to create immersive, interactive simulations that offer students and junior doctors hands-on practice with cardiovascular conditions in a risk-free, virtual setting.

In the VR environment, students can interact with high-fidelity 3D models of the heart and other cardiovascular structures. They can visualize real-time ECG readings, listen to heart sounds, and manipulate rotatable heart models. This dynamic setup allows students to practice diagnosing heart diseases such as bradycardia, tachycardia, and cardiac murmurs. For instance, students can learn how to place stethoscopes on virtual patients, listen to different heart sounds, and compare these sounds with visual ECG readings, all within a fully simulated environment. By replicating real-life scenarios, this VR solution enhances students' diagnostic skills without the risk of harming real patients.

The AR component further enhances the learning experience by integrating real-time diagnostic tools like ECG and angiogram scans into the environment. AR applications can superimpose 3D models of the heart onto real-world settings, allowing students to use their smartphones or AR devices to scan real ECG data and visualize the corresponding heart conditions in real time. This environment also includes interactive quizzes and machine learning algorithms that assist students in verifying their diagnoses, building their confidence in diagnosing heart conditions and interpreting angiogram results without the need for invasive procedures.

The VR angiogram environment is particularly useful for teaching coronary angiography, a critical procedure for diagnosing coronary artery diseases. By offering an immersive learning environment that simulates coronary angiograms, students can practice identifying blockages or abnormalities in the coronary arteries with higher accuracy. The simulation provides interactive feedback to guide students, helping them to understand how different patterns in angiograms can represent various diagnoses.

Overall, VascularVision transforms medical education by bridging the gap between theory and practice. It offers an intuitive, realistic, and engaging way for students to learn, significantly improving their diagnostic proficiency and preparing them for real-world clinical challenges. The use of advanced technologies not only makes the learning process more engaging but also ensures that students are better equipped to handle the complexities of cardiovascular diseases in a clinical setting.

Our Solution

Research Objectives

VR Cardiac Auscultation Environment: Create a virtual reality (VR) environment that allows students to experience and engage with heart rhythms and sounds, especially those associated with diseases like supraventricular tachycardia (SVT). Students will learn how to recognise cardiovascular diseases based on sound and vibration by simulating real-life auscultation settings in the virtual reality environment. Augmented Reality (AR) Cardiac Auscultation Application: Create an augmented reality tool that simulates heartbeats and provides likely diagnosis ECG reports to assist physicians and students in improving their accuracy while evaluating cardiac problems using auscultation. By serving as a second source of confirmation for physicians, this tool seeks to increase diagnostic accuracy. Virtual Reality (VR) Angiogram Environment: Create a virtual reality environment that mimics coronary angiography so that students can see and examine different artery blockages and associated conditions. By improving comprehension of angiographic outputs, this seeks to improve diagnostic abilities. AR Angiogram Application: Make an augmented reality app that can scan coronary angiography and compare the results to references that have been approved by cardiologists. This helps students and medical professionals make better decisions by assisting in the identification of problems.

Methodology

This research follows a design and development approach to create an Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality Coronary Angiogram Application for medical education. The process involves expert consultations and iterative development using YOLO-based image detection, AR frameworks like Unity and ARCore, and VR for immersive learning experiences. Usability testing with medical students and junior doctors will evaluate the app's effectiveness, while cardiologists will validate its accuracy in interpreting ECG and angiogram data. Ethical considerations, including data anonymization and informed consent, will be prioritized throughout the study.

Technologies Used

Our platform utilizes cutting-edge technologies such as Unity for 3D modeling, ARCore for AR functionality, and machine learning for enhanced interactivity and diagnostics.
